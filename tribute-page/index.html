Kofi Annan

UN Secretary General (1997 - 2006)

Early years and Education

Kofi Annan was born on April 8, 1938, in Kumasi, Ghana. He went on to pursue higher education, attending four different colleges: Kumasi College of Science and Technology; Macalester College in St. Paul, Minnesota; Graduate Institute of International Studies in Geneva, Switzerland; and the MIT Sloan School of Management in Cambridge, Massachusetts. He earned a number of degrees, including a Master of Science, and studied international relations.

Career

Kofi Annan began his career with the UN as a budget officer for the World Health Orgainization in Geneva in 1962. He spent almost his entire career with the UN, serving in several administrative posts. On march 1, 1993, he was elevated to undersecretary-general for peacekeeping operations. He later became the secretary-general in 1997 and served in that capacity for two terms. In 2006 Annan's term ended, and he was succeeded by Ban Ki-Moon. After he left the UN, Annan founded the "Kofi Annan Foundation", a non-profit organization that promotes peace, sustainable development, human rights, and the rule of law.

Personal Life

In 1965, Kofi Annan married Titi Alakija, a Nigerian woman from an aristocratic family. Several years later they had a daughter, Ama, and later a son, Kojo. The couple separated in the late 1970s, and divorced in 1983. In 1984, Annan married Nane Annan, a Swedish lawyer at the UN and a maternal half-niece of diplomat Raoul Wallenberg. She has a daughter, Nina, from a previous marriage. His brother, Kobina Annan served as Ghana's ambassador to Morocco.

Death

Kofi Annan died on the morning of 18 August 2018 in Bern, Switzerland, at the age of 80 after a short illness. His body was returned to his native country, Ghana from Geneva.
